How to do the pythagorean theorem.

By:Luka Felbapov


The pythagorean theorem was invented by a man named pythagoras back in 500 bce. it is used to tell the length of a right triangles hypothenuse by squaring side a (The triangles shortest side) and adding it to side b squared (side b is the triangles middle length side).
the first step to doing the pythagorean theorem is to add a squared to b squared, the sum of these two numbers is equal to c squared, finally you want to find the square root of c squared. The square root of c squared is equal to c which is the length of your right triangles hypothenuse. Congratulations! You have sucessfully done the pythagorean theorem.
